Reflection

So far, in most cases, I have been pleased with the results of my quizzes, as they have shown that students are effectively learning the material. Sometimes, however, they have also helped to highlight students who are struggling. In Latin I, there were a few students that I found to be struggling with the material. One of these students did do well on the next quiz, after I had some one-on-one instructional time with her. For another, student, it became clear to me that she was not studying (given that she was able to recall very little of the vocabulary). Through informal, formative assessment, I noticed that students in Latin III struggled with pronouns, so that is why I decided to give the Pronoun Quiz linked below. In hindsight, I think I also should have spent more time in class reviewing pronouns as the grades on the Pronoun Quiz were generally not very good (in spite of the detailed study guide I included on Schoology). I also realize now how important it is to plan out summative assessments in advance, as I found with my Latin V class that it was difficult to ensure that my mid-unit and unit superquizzes/exams were at an appropriate difficulty level.
