Introduction

While I have not yet taught high school students, I will be student teaching in the fall, and I did teach Latin I to undergraduate students as a graduate assistant.

Here is a summary of my educational background:

  • Albright College - Will be earning my teaching certification (both Latin and Language Arts) at the end of 2025 by taking grad classes and student teaching
  • Kent State University - Graduated in 2023 with an MA in Latin
  • Boston University - Graduated in 2021 with a Master of Sacred Theology (STM)
  • Regent College - Graduated in 2019 with an MA in theological studies
  • Taylor University - Graduated in 2010 with a BS in computer science

Basically, I have a love of languages and of ancient Greco-Roman history and culture that I look forward to sharing with my students.


My Journey with Educational Technology

I created this website for my Educational Technology class at Albright College. As I am a computer science major who worked in web development for six years, I went into this class being fairly comfortable with technology. Nonetheless, I was not familiar with much of the technology that is commonly used in educational contexts. Through this class, I became more familiar with the following technologies:

  • ChatGPT - experimented with it and read articles about the controversy surrounding it in educational contexts
  • Nearpod - used to create an interactive presentation for students
  • Screencastify - used to create several videos
  • Google Classrooms - used to create a learning module
  • Google Maps - specifically creating maps with it
  • Google Slides - though I still prefer PowerPoint

In addition to these technologies, there were other technologies that were introduced that I did not look into. However, I copied pasted links relating to these technologies to a document so that I can potentially come back to them later.
